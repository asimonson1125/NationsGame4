{% endmacro %} {# ── Divisions & Mobilization ── #} {{ guide_section('divisions', 'fas fa-flag', 'Divisions & Mobilization') }}

Units must be organized into divisions before they can act. A division groups your units together for deployment, combat, and administration.

  • Demobilized — division is at rest. Units recover but cannot be deployed.
  • Mobilizing — division is being called up. Cannot act yet.
  • Mobilized — division is ready. Can be deployed to wars, missions, or peacekeeping.

You can rename or disband a division at any time (disbanding moves units to reserve). A division in combat cannot be disbanded.

Demobilization is blocked while a division is set as defensive, traveling, or in active combat.

{{ end_section() }} {# ── Defensive Divisions ── #} {{ guide_section('defense', 'fas fa-shield-halved', 'Defensive Divisions') }}

Each nation can designate one defensive division. This division guards your nation against incoming attacks from any active war — it applies nation-wide, not per-war.

To assign a defensive division, open a mobilized division and click Set Defense. The division will be marked with a shield icon.

  • A defensive division cannot be demobilized while assigned. Relieve it first.
  • A defensive division cannot be deployed offensively to wars, missions, or peacekeeping.
  • If you have no defensive division when an attack arrives, it resolves as an unopposed victory for your enemy.

To remove a division from defense, click Relieve from Defense on that division's panel.

{{ end_section() }} {# ── Deployment ── #} {{ guide_section('deploy', 'fas fa-paper-plane', 'Deployment & Travel') }}

To send an attack in a war, open the war page and select a division from the Deploy Attack panel. The division will travel for 24 hours before the battle resolves.

Only divisions that meet all of the following are eligible to deploy:

  • Mobilized
  • Not currently set as your defensive division
  • Not already traveling to any war
  • Not already in active combat
  • Has at least one living unit

You may cancel a deployment while it is still EN ROUTE. Once it arrives, battle begins automatically.

The same deployment eligibility rules apply to peacekeeping and missions.

{{ end_section() }} {# ── Wars ── #} {{ guide_section('wars', 'fas fa-person-rifle', 'Wars') }}

Wars are declared from an enemy's nation profile page. You must supply a war name and a casus belli (justification) that will appear publicly on the war page.

Scoring

Each battle in a war awards a victory point to the winning side. The score is tracked on the war page.

Settlements

  • White Peace — either side may offer at any time. If accepted, the war ends with no transfers. An offer can be withdrawn before the opponent accepts.
  • War Compensation — available when you hold a lead of 3 or more victories. Claims 35% of the enemy's resource stockpiles and ends the war.
  • Annexation — requires 3+ total victory lead AND 3+ offensive victories. Transfers 20% of the enemy's land and population and ends the war.

Unopposed Attacks

If an attack arrives and your enemy has no mobilized, non-combat defensive division, a battle is recorded instantly as an unopposed victory for the attacker. It appears in battle logs with the defender listed as (Unopposed).
